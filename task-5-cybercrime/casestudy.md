# Case Study: UPI/Online Payment Fraud

UPI or online payment fraud happens when someone tricks you into sending money from your bank account. Usually, it starts with a call, message, or email that looks like it is from your bank. They often say something urgent, like “your transaction failed” or “your account has suspicious activity,” and ask you to share OTPs, UPI PIN, or other details.

Here’s how it usually happens:
1. You get a message or call asking for sensitive information.
2. You share your UPI PIN or OTP without checking.
3. The scammer uses this to transfer money from your account.
4. Once the money is gone, it is very hard to get back.

Students and young people who are new to online payments are often targeted. Losing money, stress, and difficulty getting it back are the main problems.

**Source:** National Cyber Crime Portal (cybercrime.gov.in) and news reports on UPI fraud in India.
