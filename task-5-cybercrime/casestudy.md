# Case Study: UPI/Online Payment Fraud

UPI or online payment fraud is a crime where scammers trick people into sending money from their bank accounts. It usually starts with a call, SMS, or email that looks like it is from your bank or a payment app. They often create a sense of urgency, like saying “Your account has suspicious activity” or “A transaction failed,” and ask you to share OTPs, UPI PIN, or other sensitive information.

Here’s how it usually happens step by step:
1. The victim receives a message or call asking to confirm account details or OTP.  
2. The victim shares the requested information without verifying the source.  
3. The fraudster uses these details to transfer money immediately from the victim’s account.  
4. Once the money is transferred, it is very difficult to recover.

College students and young adults who are new to online payments are often targeted. The scammers create messages that look very official, so even careful users can be tricked. Losing money, facing emotional stress, and the difficulty of recovering the funds are common consequences for the victims.  

This type of fraud is becoming more common as more people use UPI apps for payments. Awareness about this crime and following safe online habits can prevent victims from losing money.

**Source:** National Cyber Crime Portal (cybercrime.gov.in) and news reports on UPI fraud in India.
